Lombok

Lombok is an island located in the Indonesian archipelago, specifically in the West Nusa Tenggara province of Indonesia. It is situated to the east of Bali and west of Sumbawa. Lombok spans an area of approximately 4,725 square kilometers and is known for its stunning natural beauty, diverse culture, and enchanting landscapes.

Geographically, Lombok is characterized by a rugged coastline, picturesque mountains, lush forests, and pristine white sandy beaches. One of its most iconic landmarks is Mount Rinjani, an active volcano and the second-highest peak in Indonesia. The mountain, with its breathtaking summit crater lake, attracts adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts from around the world.

Lombok is renowned for its pristine beaches, many of which boast crystal-clear turquoise waters and vibrant coral reefs. Kuta Beach, Selong Belanak, Tanjung Aan, and Pink Beach are just a few of the island's idyllic coastal destinations. These beaches offer opportunities for swimming, sunbathing, snorkeling, and surfing, making Lombok a haven for water sports and beach lovers.
